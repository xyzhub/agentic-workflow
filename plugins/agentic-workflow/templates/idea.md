# {{PROJECT_NAME}} — Idea & Validation (V0)

The cheapest place to kill a bad idea is here, in prose. Fill this before writing
code; the exit gate is a human go/no-go.

## The problem
_Who has it, how painful is it, and how do they cope today?_

## Who pays
_The buyer (may differ from the user). What would make it a must-have, not a
nice-to-have?_

## Why now
_What changed — technology, cost, regulation, behavior — that makes this viable
today when it wasn't before?_

## The riskiest assumption
_The one belief that, if false, kills the whole thing. Not "will people like it"
— the specific load-bearing assumption._

## Cheapest test of that assumption
_The fastest, smallest way to find out if the riskiest assumption holds —
before building. A conversation, a landing page, a manual concierge run._

## Kill criteria
_What result from the test means STOP. Decide this now, while you can still be
honest with yourself._

## Rough shape (one paragraph, no detail)
_If the assumption holds, what's the smallest thing that delivers the core value?
This becomes the seed of the V1 MVP scope — keep it a paragraph, not a spec._

---
_Exit V0 → V1 when the human gives an explicit go, informed by the test result._
